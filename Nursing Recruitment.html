Nursing Recruitment

Overview

Nursing job chances in the UK are generally quite promising. The demand for qualified nurses in the UK remains consistently high due to factors such as an aging population, increased healthcare needs, and workforce gaps. Nursing is classified as a shortage occupation in the UK, which means that there are more job opportunities available for qualified nurses from both the UK and overseas. The National Health Service (NHS) is the largest employer of nurses in the UK. The NHS regularly recruits nurses across various specialties and settings, including hospitals, community healthcare, mental health, and primary care. In addition to the NHS, there are also numerous opportunities within the private healthcare sector, including private hospitals, clinics, and nursing homes. These organizations often have their own recruitment processes and offer competitive benefits packages. There is a demand for nurses with specialized skills and expertise in areas such as General wards, ICU, NICU, PICU, Emergency, Theatre, Critical care, Paediatric nursing, Mental health, Oncology, and Community nursing. Nurses with advanced qualifications and experience in these specialties may have even more job prospects.

BECOMING A NURSE IN THE UK

1.Demonstrate English Language Proficiency

IELTS with minimum score of 7 in speaking, listening and reading and 6.5 in writing or OET with minimum grade of B in speaking, listening and reading and C+ in writing

2. Complete NMC Eligibility & Qualification Application

Applicant will create online account and complete eligibility and qualification application. NMC will confirm eligibility for registration within 14 days

3. Take CBT (Computer Based Test)

CBT assures the NMC that the applicant has the right knowledge and skills to practice nursing in the UK.

4. Attend a direct or online interview arranged by us to secure your job offer.

Attend Direct or Online Interview & Secure Job Offer Direct/Online interview arranged by Elizabeth International UK. Secure job offer and complete pre-employment checks.

5. Secure COS from the Employer (Certificate of Sponsorship)

Employers assign the CoS once the CBT exam is cleared. CoS is used for the Tier-2 visa application.

6. Submit Tier 2 Health & Care Worker Visa Application

The applicant will submit their Tier 2 Visa application and provide a biometric application visa within 3 weeks

7. Travel to UK

We will arrange a one-way flight to the UK, airport pickup, temporary accommodation, and orientation.

8. Clear the Objective Structured Clinical Exam (OSCE)

OSCE will assess applicant’s clinical knowledge. Results emailed within 5 working days from test date.

9.Complete NMC Registration

Submit health, character and language evidence. NMC will assess the application in 35 days. Then you will receive Registration number from NMC (PIN)
